The problem

A resource this large should not be invisible.

Rice straw, wheat stubble, sugarcane trash, cotton stalk — the leftovers of India's fields are a genuine energy resource. But the data describing them is fragmented across ministries, PDFs and state tables, published in formats no one can query.

Because no one counts it well, much of it is burned in the open every season, wasting energy and fouling the air. IBIP exists to make that resource legible: collect the data automatically, estimate what is recoverable, and put it on a map.
